Plant Care & Maintenance

How do I care for lilies in Trinidad's climate?

Lilies thrive in Trinidad's tropical climate but need proper care. Plant them in well-draining soil with partial sunlight (4-6 hours daily), However they strive really well in direct sun.. Water regularly but avoid waterlogging. In rainy seasons, reduce watering frequency. Apply organic fertilizer every 4-6 weeks during growing season.

What's the best way to plant lotus species?

Lotus plants need a water environment. Use a large container about 30 - 40 inches diameter for one plant. There are also small plants that can be planted into a small barrel (at least 20-30 gallons) filled with heavy loam soil. Plant the tuber horizontally about 2-3 inches deep. Keep the container in full sunlight (6+ hours daily) and maintain 4-6 inches of water above soil level. Fertilize with aquatic plant fertilizer during growing season.

How often should I water exotic ornamental plants?

Watering frequency depends on the plant species and season. As a general rule, check soil moisture regularly. Water when the top 1-2 inches of soil feel dry. Most exotic plants prefer consistent moisture but not soggy soil. During dry seasons, increase watering frequency; during rainy seasons, reduce it.

What fertilizers do you recommend for lilies?

We recommend our specially formulated Lily Boost fertilizer (available in our store). Alternatively, use a balanced 10-10-10 NPK fertilizer, these can be folded or wrapped in biodegradable material (napkin, etc), and must be sunk into the soil. What is NPK?

NPK stands for three primary nutrients found in most fertilizers:

  • N - Nitrogen: Essential for leaf growth and overall plant health.
  • P - Phosphorus: Important for root development and flower/fruit production.
  • K - Potassium: Helps with overall plant vigor and disease resistance
