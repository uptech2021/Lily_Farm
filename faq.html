Rishi's Lily Farm & Exotic Plants - FAQ

Water Lily FAQs

How often should I fertilize my water lilies?

Water lilies should be fertilized every 3–4 weeks during the growing season using aquatic fertilizer tablets placed in the soil near the roots.

Do water lilies need full sun?

Yes. Most water lilies require at least 6 hours of direct sunlight daily to bloom properly.

Can water lilies grow in moving water?

Water lilies prefer still water. Strong currents or splashing can prevent them from blooming and damage their leaves.

How deep should I plant my water lilies?

Hardy and tropical water lilies typically grow best when planted 12–24 inches below the surface, depending on variety.

Why are my lily leaves turning yellow?

Yellowing leaves can result from overfertilizing, lack of nutrients, low sunlight, or natural leaf aging. Remove old leaves regularly.

Exotic Plant FAQs

How often should exotic plants be watered?

Most tropical plants prefer consistently moist but not waterlogged soil. Water when the top inch of soil feels dry.

Do exotic plants need humidity?

Yes. Many exotic plants such as Heliconias, Champaca, and Amaryllis thrive in moderate to high humidity environments.

Can I grow exotic plants indoors?

Some species like Amaryllis and certain orchids can be grown indoors, but most tropical plants prefer outdoor environments with bright, indirect light.

What type of soil is best for exotic plants?

A well-draining soil mixed with organic matter such as compost or coco peat helps exotic plants grow healthy and strong.

How do I prevent pests on exotic plants?

Regularly inspect leaves, improve air circulation, avoid overwatering, and treat pests early using neem oil or insecticidal soap.
